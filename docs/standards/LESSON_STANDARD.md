# LESSON STANDARD

> **Document ID:** OA-STD-001
> **Version:** 1.0.0
> **Status:** Active
> **Owner:** Oravil Academy
> **Applies To:** All lessons within Oravil Academy

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the mandatory structure and quality standards for every lesson published within Oravil Academy.

Its purpose is to ensure consistency, practical value, and a unified learning experience across all learning paths.

Every lesson MUST comply with this standard.

---

# 2. Objectives

Every lesson must:

- Teach one clear concept or skill.
- Produce one measurable learning outcome.
- Include practical application.
- Prepare the learner for real-world work.
- Build upon previous lessons without unnecessary repetition.

---

# 3. Lesson Structure

Every lesson MUST follow the structure below.

---

# 3.1 Lesson Information

Include:

- Lesson Title
- Learning Path
- Phase
- Module
- Lesson Number
- Difficulty
- Estimated Duration
- Prerequisites

---

# 3.2 Learning Objectives

Define what the learner will be able to accomplish after completing the lesson.

Objectives must be measurable.

Example:

- Explain...
- Configure...
- Analyze...
- Build...
- Publish...

---

# 3.3 Business Context

Explain why this topic matters in a real business environment.

Answer:

Why would a company pay someone to know this?

---

# 3.4 Core Concepts

Introduce the required theory.

Keep explanations practical.

Avoid unnecessary academic detail.

---

# 3.5 Professional Thinking

Describe how experienced professionals approach this topic.

Highlight the difference between:

- Beginner thinking
- Professional thinking

---

# 3.6 Inside Oravil

If applicable, explain how this knowledge is applied inside Oravil workflows.

Examples:

- Approval process
- Team collaboration
- Client communication
- Internal standards

---

# 3.7 Practical Example

Provide one realistic business example.

The example should represent a real client or business scenario whenever possible.

---

# 3.8 Hands-on Workshop

Guide the learner through a complete practical exercise.

The learner must produce something tangible.

Examples:

- Campaign
- Dashboard
- Persona
- SEO Audit
- Content Plan

---

# 3.9 Assignment

Provide an individual assignment.

The assignment should reinforce the lesson without repeating the workshop.

---

# 3.10 Common Mistakes

List the most common beginner mistakes.

Explain how to avoid them.

---

# 3.11 Troubleshooting

Document common issues and practical solutions.

---

# 3.12 Knowledge Check

Verify that the learner can:

- Explain
- Apply
- Analyze
- Execute

This section is not limited to multiple-choice questions.

---

# 3.13 Lesson Summary

Summarize the key takeaways.

Do not introduce new concepts.

---

# 3.14 Next Lesson

Explain how the current lesson connects to the next lesson.

Maintain continuity across the learning path.

---

# 4. Lesson Deliverable

Every lesson should produce at least one meaningful output.

Examples include:

- Document
- Report
- Spreadsheet
- Campaign
- Design
- Workflow
- Dashboard

A lesson without a deliverable should be considered incomplete unless explicitly justified.

---

# 5. Writing Standards

Lessons should be:

- Clear
- Practical
- Concise
- Action-oriented
- Consistent

Avoid:

- Marketing language
- Unnecessary theory
- Repetition
- Vendor bias
- Tool-specific dependency when teaching transferable concepts

---

# 6. Quality Checklist

Before publishing a lesson, verify:

- [ ] Lesson structure is complete.
- [ ] Objectives are measurable.
- [ ] Business context is included.
- [ ] Practical example is realistic.
- [ ] Workshop is executable.
- [ ] Assignment is included.
- [ ] Common mistakes are documented.
- [ ] Knowledge check validates understanding.
- [ ] Deliverable is defined.
- [ ] Summary is complete.
- [ ] Lesson is consistent with the Academy Constitution.

---

# 7. Definition of Done

A lesson is considered complete only when:

- It follows this standard.
- It achieves its learning objectives.
- It includes practical application.
- It produces a measurable learner outcome.
- It is ready to be delivered without additional clarification.

Completion is measured by learner capability, not lesson length.
