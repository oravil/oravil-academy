# MVP Product Brief

**Document ID:** OA-MVP-001
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Status:** Approved
**Date:** 2026-07-16

---

## Product Vision

Version 0.1 of Oravil Academy exists to validate one question: does the Oravil learning methodology produce measurable comprehension and practical skill in real learners. The MVP delivers Phase 0, Module 1 — The Digital Marketing Landscape — to a small group of target learners, collects structured completion and feedback data, and determines whether the methodology is sound before any further content is produced or infrastructure is built.

---

## Product Goal

At least 70% of enrolled learners complete all four lessons and submit all four assignments within the defined testing window.

---

## Problem Statement

Junior marketers and career-changers entering digital marketing lack a structured starting point that bridges conceptual orientation and practical thinking. Existing resources either present isolated definitions without professional context, or jump directly into tools and tactics before establishing mental models.

The result is learners who can recall definitions but cannot apply concepts to real business scenarios. They struggle to distinguish channels, understand how media types relate, or explain their thinking to a colleague.

Oravil Academy proposes a methodology that pairs business context with structured reflection and practical deliverables in every lesson. The MVP tests whether this methodology produces a meaningfully different learning outcome compared to the learner's baseline — measured through assignment quality and self-reported comprehension.

---

## Success Criteria

1. 70% or more of enrolled learners complete all four lessons.
2. 70% or more of enrolled learners submit all four lesson assignments.
3. Submitted assignments demonstrate correct application of lesson concepts as assessed against the rubric defined in each Lesson Brief.
4. 80% or more of learners rate the learning experience 4 or higher out of 5 on a post-module survey.
5. Qualitative feedback from at least 50% of learners is specific enough to inform content revisions.

---

## Target Audience

The first users are adults with no formal digital marketing training who are either beginning a marketing career, transitioning from a non-marketing role, or taking on marketing responsibilities at a small business. They are comfortable reading professional documents, motivated to complete structured tasks, and willing to provide feedback. They are not enrolled in formal education and are not experts in the subject matter.

---

## In Scope

- Delivery of Phase 0, Module 1 — four lessons in structured markdown format.
- A mechanism for learners to read each lesson in sequence.
- A mechanism for learners to submit lesson assignments.
- A post-module survey collecting completion data and satisfaction ratings.
- A process for reviewing and scoring submitted assignments against Lesson Brief rubrics.
- Structured feedback collection at the end of the module.

---

## Out of Scope

The following will not be built for Version 0.1:

- Certificates or credentials of any kind.
- Gamification, points, badges, or progress rewards.
- A course marketplace or monetisation layer.
- An AI tutor or AI-assisted feedback system.
- Community features, discussion forums, or peer interaction.
- Multiple learning paths or any content beyond Phase 0, Module 1.
- Advanced analytics, learner dashboards, or reporting infrastructure.
- Mobile applications.
- Assessments, quizzes, or automated grading.
- Account management, subscription, or billing.

---

## User Journey

1. Learner receives access to Module 1 through the delivery channel established for the MVP.
2. Learner reads the module overview and understands the four-lesson structure and the module deliverable.
3. Learner reads Lesson 1 — What Is Digital Marketing in full.
4. Learner completes the Lesson 1 Workshop activity.
5. Learner submits the Lesson 1 Assignment deliverable.
6. Learner reads Lesson 2 — Digital vs. Traditional Marketing in full.
7. Learner completes the Lesson 2 Workshop activity.
8. Learner submits the Lesson 2 Assignment deliverable.
9. Learner reads Lesson 3 — Core Digital Marketing Channels in full.
10. Learner completes the Lesson 3 Workshop activity.
11. Learner submits the Lesson 3 Assignment deliverable.
12. Learner reads Lesson 4 — Digital Marketing Careers in full.
13. Learner completes the Lesson 4 Workshop activity and finalises the Digital Marketing Landscape Map.
14. Learner submits the Lesson 4 Assignment deliverable.
15. Learner completes the post-module survey.
16. Learner receives confirmation that Module 1 is complete.

---

## MVP Screens

- Module Overview
- Lesson View
- Assignment Submission
- Module Complete
- Post-Module Survey

---

## Definition of Success

Version 0.2 is approved when the Version 0.1 testing window closes and the data shows that the methodology works: learners complete the content, submit assignments that demonstrate conceptual understanding, and report a satisfactory learning experience. If completion rates fall below threshold, or if submitted assignments indicate that learners cannot apply concepts to new scenarios, the methodology is revised before any further content is produced. Version 0.2 does not begin on a schedule — it begins when Version 0.1 data supports it.
