## Purpose

Provide a high-level orientation to the major categories of digital marketing channels — search, social media, email, display, content, and affiliate — so the learner can identify what each category is, what business purpose it serves, and when it is generally appropriate, before undertaking detailed channel study in later phases.

---

## Learning Objectives

- Name the primary digital marketing channel categories.
- Describe the primary business purpose of each channel category in one sentence.
- Distinguish between channel categories based on how they reach audiences.
- Identify which channel category is most appropriate for a given high-level business objective.
- Classify a set of real-world marketing activities by channel category.

---

## Key Concepts

- Search channel
- Social media channel
- Email marketing
- Display advertising
- Content marketing
- Affiliate and partnership marketing
- Channel categories vs. individual platforms
- Inbound vs. outbound channels
- Pull vs. push marketing
- Channel-objective alignment

---

## Business Context

A practitioner presented with a client brief must be able to identify which channel categories are relevant before any detailed planning begins. Without a working map of the channel landscape, channel selection defaults to familiarity rather than fit. Understanding what each channel category does — at an orientation level — is the prerequisite for every planning and strategy decision that follows.

---

## Professional Competency

The learner can identify all major digital marketing channel categories, describe the role each plays in a marketing program, distinguish between inbound and outbound channel types, and make an informed first-pass channel recommendation for a given business objective — without requiring detailed channel knowledge.

---

## Deliverable

A channel classification table added to the Module 1 Landscape Map listing each major digital marketing channel category, its primary reach mechanism (inbound or outbound), and its primary business purpose. The table must cover a minimum of six channel categories and be written in the learner's own words.

---

## Assessment Method

The channel classification table is evaluated against a rubric covering: correct identification of at least six channel categories, accurate classification of reach mechanism for each, accurate one-sentence purpose statement for each, and internal consistency across the table. Pass requires meeting all four rubric criteria.
