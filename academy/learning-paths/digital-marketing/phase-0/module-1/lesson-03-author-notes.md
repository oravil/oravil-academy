# Author Notes

> **Lesson:** Core Digital Marketing Channels
> **Phase:** Phase 0 — Foundations
> **Module:** Module 1 — The Digital Marketing Landscape

---

## Writing Objective

Write a lesson that gives the learner a clear, practical orientation to the major digital marketing channel categories — what each one is, what business purpose it serves, and how channels differ from one another — without entering the implementation detail that belongs to later phases.

---

## Key Messages

- The digital marketing landscape comprises distinct channel categories, each serving a different business function.
- Channels are selected based on business objectives and audience behavior, not familiarity or popularity.
- Understanding the channel map is a prerequisite for every planning decision that follows.
- No single channel category is universally superior — each has specific contexts where it performs best.
- Execution knowledge for each channel belongs to later phases; this lesson provides orientation only.

---

## Definitions

**Channel category:** A grouping of digital marketing channels that share a common reach mechanism and primary business function (e.g., search, social, email).

**Inbound channel:** A channel through which the audience initiates contact or discovery (e.g., search, content).

**Outbound channel:** A channel through which the marketer initiates contact with the audience (e.g., display advertising, email to a cold list).

---

## Things to Emphasize

- The distinction between channel categories and individual platforms — categories are stable; platforms change.
- The difference between inbound and outbound reach mechanisms as a practical decision framework.
- That channel categories are tools, not strategies — the strategy determines which tools are used.
- The learner should leave with a mental map, not a capability to execute.
- Every channel category introduced must be linked to at least one business purpose.

---

## Things to Avoid

- Do not teach SEO techniques, keyword research, or on-page optimization.
- Do not explain how to set up or run campaigns on any platform.
- Do not compare specific platforms against each other (Google vs. Meta, etc.).
- Do not introduce content strategy, editorial planning, or copywriting.
- Do not introduce analytics, attribution, or measurement frameworks.

---

## Scope Boundaries

### Included

- Names and one-sentence descriptions of each major channel category.
- Primary business purpose of each channel category.
- Inbound vs. outbound classification.
- High-level guidance on which channel categories suit which business objectives.
- Channel categories as a map, not a specification.

### Not Included

- Implementation details for any channel.
- Platform-specific features, interfaces, or ad formats.
- Campaign structure, bidding, targeting setup.
- Analytics or performance measurement methodology.
- Marketing automation, CRM, or lifecycle marketing.
- Career specializations by channel (Lesson 4).

---

## Required Terminology

- Channel category
- Search marketing
- Social media marketing
- Email marketing
- Display advertising
- Content marketing
- Affiliate marketing
- Inbound channel
- Outbound channel
- Channel-objective alignment
