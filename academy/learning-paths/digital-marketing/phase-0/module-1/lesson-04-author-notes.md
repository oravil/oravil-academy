# Author Notes

> **Lesson:** Digital Marketing Careers
> **Phase:** Phase 0 — Foundations
> **Module:** Module 1 — The Digital Marketing Landscape

---

## Writing Objective

Write a lesson that gives the learner a clear professional orientation to the major roles within a digital marketing team — what each role owns, how roles collaborate, and what the generalist-to-specialist career path looks like — without teaching the execution skills that belong to each specialization.

---

## Key Messages

- Digital marketing is practiced by a team of complementary roles, not a single all-knowing practitioner.
- Every role has a defined ownership area that maps to a channel category or function.
- Generalists provide breadth; specialists provide depth — both are valued at different career stages.
- Roles collaborate through handoffs, shared objectives, and integrated program ownership.
- Understanding the team structure is a professional prerequisite before any specialization begins.

---

## Definitions

**Generalist:** A digital marketing practitioner with working knowledge across multiple channel categories and functions, typically found at early career stages or in smaller teams.

**Specialist:** A digital marketing practitioner with deep execution expertise in a specific channel or function, typically found in larger teams or agencies.

**T-shaped marketer:** A practitioner with broad awareness across the digital marketing discipline and deep expertise in one or more specific areas.

---

## Things to Emphasize

- The team structure exists because no single person can execute all channels at expert level simultaneously.
- Role collaboration is as important as individual competency — understanding what others do is a professional requirement.
- The learner's career path will likely start as a generalist and narrow toward a specialism over time.
- Every role in the team traces back to one or more of the channel categories introduced in Lesson 3.
- Understanding the full team map makes the learner a better collaborator, regardless of their eventual specialism.

---

## Things to Avoid

- Do not teach the execution skills of any role — SEO, paid media, email, analytics, and content production belong to later phases.
- Do not compare salaries, job market conditions, or employment demand.
- Do not recommend certifications, courses, or external training programs.
- Do not describe hiring processes, job descriptions, or recruitment criteria.
- Do not turn the lesson into career advice — it is a professional orientation, not a career guide.

---

## Scope Boundaries

### Included

- Names and primary responsibilities of the major digital marketing roles.
- Distinction between generalist and specialist practitioners.
- Overview of in-house vs. agency team structures at a conceptual level.
- How roles collaborate on a program or campaign.
- Typical career progression from generalist entry level to specialist or strategic senior roles.

### Not Included

- Execution skills, tools, or techniques for any role.
- Salary benchmarks or labor market data.
- Certifications, qualifications, or recommended training paths.
- Hiring processes or job application guidance.
- Platform-specific skill requirements.
- Career advice beyond the scope of professional orientation.
