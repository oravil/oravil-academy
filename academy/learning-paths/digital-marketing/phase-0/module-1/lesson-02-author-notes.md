# Author Notes

> **Lesson:** Digital vs. Traditional Marketing
> **Phase:** Phase 0 — Foundations
> **Module:** Module 1 — The Digital Marketing Landscape

---

## Writing Objective

Write a lesson that equips the learner to compare digital and traditional marketing objectively across structural dimensions — reach, measurement, cost, and audience control — and to reason about which approach fits a given business context, without advocating for either model.

---

## Key Messages

- Digital and traditional marketing are structurally different, not hierarchically ranked.
- Traditional marketing retains clear advantages in specific contexts.
- Digital marketing's primary structural advantage is measurability and audience precision.
- Professional practitioners choose channels based on business objectives, not preference.
- An integrated approach is often more effective than either in isolation.

---

## Definitions

**Traditional marketing:** Marketing activity delivered through offline channels — print, broadcast, outdoor, and direct mail — that reaches audiences through mass or semi-targeted distribution.

**Integrated marketing:** A coordinated approach in which digital and traditional channels are used together to deliver a consistent message and support a unified business objective.

**Reach model:** The method by which a marketing channel delivers messages to an audience — either broadcast (mass, untargeted) or targeted (defined audience parameters).

---

## Things to Emphasize

- The comparison must be objective — both models have genuine strengths and genuine limitations.
- Measurement capability is the most operationally significant difference between the two models.
- Channel selection is always a business decision, not a technology decision.
- Integrated programs are the norm in professional practice, not the exception.
- The fitness studio scenario from Lesson 1 is a suitable anchor for applying the comparison.

---

## Things to Avoid

- Do not present digital marketing as superior to traditional marketing in all cases.
- Do not explain any individual digital channel in depth — SEO, Google Ads, Meta Ads, email marketing are out of scope.
- Do not introduce analytics frameworks, attribution models, or measurement tools.
- Do not compare specific advertising platforms against each other.
- Do not introduce audience personas or targeting methodology — that belongs to Module 5.

---

## Scope Boundaries

### Included

- Structural comparison of digital and traditional marketing across defined dimensions.
- Contexts in which traditional marketing is the stronger choice.
- Contexts in which digital marketing is the stronger choice.
- The case for an integrated approach.
- How professionals frame the choice between approaches.

### Not Included

- Deep-dives into any specific digital channel.
- Platform comparisons (Google vs. Meta, etc.).
- Measurement tools or analytics setup.
- Audience targeting methodology.
- Campaign planning or budget allocation (covered in Phase 5).
- Career paths (Lesson 4).
