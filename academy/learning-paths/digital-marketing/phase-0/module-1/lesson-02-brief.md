## Purpose

Establish a clear structural comparison between digital and traditional marketing — covering reach models, measurement capability, cost structure, and audience control — so the learner can assess both approaches and reason about when each is appropriate in a real business context.

---

## Learning Objectives

- Identify the structural differences between digital and traditional marketing.
- Explain the measurement advantage digital marketing holds over traditional marketing.
- Describe scenarios in which traditional marketing remains the stronger choice.
- Describe scenarios in which digital marketing is the stronger choice.
- Articulate how both approaches can coexist within a single marketing strategy.

---

## Key Concepts

- Traditional marketing channels (print, broadcast, outdoor, direct mail)
- Digital marketing channels (search, social, email, display)
- Reach models: mass reach vs. targeted reach
- Measurement: proxy metrics vs. direct performance data
- Cost structure: fixed vs. variable spend
- Audience control: demographic targeting vs. broadcast
- Speed to market
- Campaign adjustability
- Integrated marketing

---

## Business Context

Organizations operating with mixed marketing budgets must make informed decisions about where to allocate spend. A practitioner who can only advocate for digital channels — without understanding traditional marketing's strengths — will give incomplete advice and lose credibility with experienced clients. The ability to compare both models objectively is a baseline professional competency.

---

## Professional Competency

The learner can compare digital and traditional marketing across multiple dimensions, identify the appropriate approach for a given business objective and context, and advise a client whose program includes both — without defaulting to a single channel type.

---

## Deliverable

A two-column comparison table added to the Module 1 Landscape Map. The table must contrast digital and traditional marketing across at least five dimensions and include one written recommendation (2–3 sentences) identifying which approach is more appropriate for the fitness studio client introduced in Lesson 1.

---

## Assessment Method

The comparison table is evaluated against a rubric covering: accuracy of each dimension, correct identification of at least one traditional marketing strength, correct identification of at least one digital marketing strength, and a written recommendation that references specific dimensions from the table. Pass requires meeting all four rubric criteria.
